# Manual Screen #2 — 16 Mons Road, Buderim

## Executive summary
Initial screen outcome: **Proceed with caution**

Reason:
- The site is materially larger than a typical suburban lot and has obvious strategic interest at screen level.
- The listing presents strong future-upside language and references existing council-approved ancillary works.
- However, the decisive planning and environmental constraints remain unverified in this pass.

## What is encouraging
- Sunshine Coast LGA location: yes
- Buderim location: yes
- **1.15ha** parcel is a strong positive signal for feasibility screening
- Existing access infrastructure noted in listing
- Listing references council approval for a granny flat and 12x12 shed, which indicates some planning history exists
- Walking distance to Buderim village is commercially positive, even though it is not itself a planning control

## What prevents a stronger Proceed result
The following checks remain unresolved:
1. current zone confirmation from authoritative council source
2. local plan / precinct confirmation
3. minimum lot size / subdivision threshold confirmation
4. flood overlay check
5. steep land / landslide check
6. biodiversity / waterways check
7. exact prior application history / approval verification

## Current assessment view
Compared with 39 Park Rd, this site is stronger on raw site area but weaker on verified planning certainty in this pass.

The large parcel is enough to justify deeper work. However, a Buderim site of this scale could be materially affected by topography, hazard overlays, vegetation constraints, or local planning controls. Those unknowns are too important to ignore.

## Recommendation
- **Current recommendation:** Proceed with caution
- **Why:** the parcel is compelling enough to justify deeper verification, but not enough is confirmed to support a confident proceed recommendation
- **Commercial usefulness:** very high as a test case because it tests whether the MVP can distinguish "large attractive site" from "verified feasible site"
